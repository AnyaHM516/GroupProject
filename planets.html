planets

Star Wars exists in a futuristic and intergalactic universe. Throughout the movies, the characters visit numerous different worlds and planets, each with their own ecosystems, political institutions, native species and norms.

planets

The Star Wars stories take place on many different planets. Some have vast deserts, others oceans and forests like earth, and others are ice worlds.

moons

The futuristic technology in Star Wars has allowed humans and aliens to colonise moons and asteroids to serve as trade hubs, mining stations or criminal hideouts.

man-made bases

Star Wars also has a number of man-made bases, from small satellite bases, to enormous plant-sized ones like the Death Star.

Tatooine
Type: Desert World
Locals: Native planet of Jawas and Tusken Raiders. Home to many miners and smugglers.
Location: In a binary star system in the Outer Rim and largely ignored by the Empire, making it an ideal place for criminal activities.
Significance: Tatooine was the home of Anakin and his mother, before he was taken on as Obi-Wan's padawan. It is later the home of Luke Skywalker, who is placed with his uncle to be safe and hidden from Darth Vader.

Naboo
Type: Gaia World
Locals: An indigineous species called Gungan, and a human population called the Naboo.
Location: Naboo is in the Mid Rim and is on important trade route making it an important and influential planet.
Significance: Naboo is the home of Padme Amidala, who would later become Anakin's wife and the mother of Luke and Leia. Naboo is an peaceful and artistic world, lead by elected child-queens, of which Padme was one during her youth.

The Death Star
Type: Man-Made base
Locals: As a man-made base, the Death Star acts as a hub of military activity for the Empire.
Location: Mobile, built in the orbit of moon Endor.
Significance: The Death Star base is mobile and equipt with a laser beam capable of destroying entire planets. It is destroyed by Luke Skywalker.

Endor
Type: Forest-Moon
Locals: Small teddy-bear like Ewoks.
Location: Outer Rim orbitting a planet of the same name. It is also the moon around which the Death Star is built.
Significance: The Empire chose it to host the construction of the Death Star, placing a protective shield around it. In Episode 6, a rebel attack on the moon and the orbitting Death Star leads to the death star's destruction and the defeat of Darth Vader.

Coruscant
Type: City-World
Locals: The capital of both the Republic and the Empire, Coruscant is is home to hundreds of species of human and aliens from all over the galaxy.
Location: Within the Core Worlds
Significance: Coruscant is the location of the Jedi Temple and the Senate. It is the hub of trade, culture and politics in the galaxy.
